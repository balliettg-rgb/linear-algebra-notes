Lecture 4: Basis and Dimension of Vector Spaces

Definition: A set of vectors {v1, v2, ..., vn} in a vector space V is called a basis if the vectors are linearly independent and span V.

Consider the matrix A where Ax = 0. The null space of A consists of all vectors x that satisfy this equation. To find the basis for the null space, we must perform Gaussian elimination to reach Reduced Row Echelon Form (RREF).

Note: The dimension of the column space of A is equal to the rank of A.

Theorem: If a vector space has a basis of n vectors, then every basis of that space must have exactly n vectors. This unique number n is called the dimension of the space, denoted dim(V).
